What are Linking Words? üîó

Linking words are like bridges between your sentences. They help your sentences hold hands and tell a complete story. Without them, your English would sound like a robot: "I went to the store. I bought milk. I came home."

With linking words, it sounds like a human: "I went to the store, and then I bought milk because we needed it at home." See the difference?
